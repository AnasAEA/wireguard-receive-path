# Article plan

## Working identity

- **Title:** Breaking Ordered-Delivery Stalls in WireGuard with Bounded Work Stealing
- **Format:** `acmart`, `nonacm,sigconf,10pt`
- **Target length:** approximately 8--10 pages of body text, plus references
- **Audience:** operating-systems and network-systems researchers

## Central research question

When WireGuard decrypts receive packets in parallel but must deliver them in
arrival order, can the blocked RX/NAPI context safely service pending decrypt
work and thereby improve throughput or CPU efficiency?

## Problem

WireGuard inserts each data packet into a per-peer queue in arrival order and
also publishes it to a device-wide decrypt ring. Per-CPU workers consume the
ring in parallel. A later packet can therefore finish before the ordered head.
The NAPI poll cannot deliver that follower, and it stops at the head while
pending work may still remain in the shared ring. This is an execution-order
inversion: the consumer required for delivery is ready to run, but the
prerequisite work is owned by a different scheduling path.

## Mechanism

`wg_steal` extends the receive poll with a bounded consumer-pull pass. When the
ordered head is still `UNCRYPTED`, the current NAPI/softirq CPU may consume jobs
from the device-wide decrypt `ptr_ring`, decrypt them synchronously, and publish
completion through WireGuard's normal per-peer completion function. The locked
ring-consume primitive transfers exclusive ownership. Delivery still advances
only from the per-peer ordered queue.

With `wg_steal=4`, one steal pass can successfully consume at most four jobs.
The pass can consume fewer; the shared ring can be empty; the ordered head can
become ready early; and the jobs need not belong to the blocked peer. Four does
not denote CPUs or threads and is not a full-poll limit. A poll that resumes
delivery can later reach another blocked head and start another pass.

## Definition of done

The article is done when:

- every quantitative claim maps to a committed raw artifact and analysis;
- exploratory knob selection is visibly separate from confirmation;
- Gate A throughput and total CPU are identified as co-primary;
- Gate A efficiency is identified as secondary and derived;
- Gate B is reported as a non-detection, with its confidence interval visible;
- no latency, equivalence, causal saturation, or production-safety claim appears;
- every figure is regenerated by `scripts/paper/make_figures.py`;
- generated PDFs have embedded fonts and remain legible in grayscale;
- the LaTeX build has no broken citations, labels, or missing figures;
- `report/` is unchanged; and
- the rendered paper has no clipping or unreadable overfull content.
