## Research objective (finalized May 18, 2026)

> **Do the work items generated by WireGuard's workqueue call blocking functions — and if so, what is the impact on scheduling latency and worker throughput?**

**What is established:**

- **EoI mechanism confirmed at source:** `napi_schedule(&peer->napi)` at `queueing.h:196` is called unconditionally after every decrypted packet. Under concurrent decryption (`WQ_PERCPU`, N CPUs), the queue head is frequently UNCRYPTED when GRO fires. GRO preempts the decrypt worker, finds nothing, exits. One core saturates at 94%; throughput collapses to 19.2% of line rate.
- **io-wq ≠ WireGuard workqueue (source-confirmed):** io-wq uses kthreads via `create_io_thread`. WireGuard uses `alloc_workqueue` / `queue_work_on`. Proxy argument is dead.
- **Blocking analysis complete:** `wg_packet_decrypt_worker` and `wg_packet_encrypt_worker` are non-blocking (pure ChaCha20-Poly1305). `wg_packet_handshake_receive_worker` and `wg_packet_handshake_send_worker` are blocking — call `down_write(&handshake->lock)` and `wait_for_random_bytes()` in `noise.c`.
- **Solution designed:** André's conditional check — read `peer->rx_queue.tail` before `napi_schedule`; skip if head is UNCRYPTED. Diff verified, source safety confirmed. See `admin/ANDRE_SOLUTION_PROPOSAL.md`.
