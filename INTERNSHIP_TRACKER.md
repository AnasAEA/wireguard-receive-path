## Research objective (finalized May 18, 2026)

> **Do the work items generated by WireGuard's workqueue call blocking functions ‚ÄĒ and if so, what is the impact on scheduling latency and worker throughput?**

**What is established:**

- **EoI mechanism confirmed at source:** `napi_schedule(&peer->napi)` at `queueing.h:196` is called unconditionally after every decrypted packet. Under concurrent decryption (`WQ_PERCPU`, N CPUs), the queue head is frequently UNCRYPTED when GRO fires. GRO preempts the decrypt worker, finds nothing, exits. One core saturates at 94%; throughput collapses to 19.2% of line rate.
- **io-wq ‚Č† WireGuard workqueue (source-confirmed):** io-wq uses kthreads via `create_io_thread`. WireGuard uses `alloc_workqueue` / `queue_work_on`. Proxy argument is dead.
- **Blocking analysis complete:** `wg_packet_decrypt_worker` and `wg_packet_encrypt_worker` are non-blocking (pure ChaCha20-Poly1305). `wg_packet_handshake_receive_worker` and `wg_packet_handshake_send_worker` are blocking ‚ÄĒ call `down_write(&handshake->lock)` and `wait_for_random_bytes()` in `noise.c`.
- **Solution designed:** Andr√©'s conditional check ‚ÄĒ read `peer->rx_queue.tail` before `napi_schedule`; skip if head is UNCRYPTED. Diff verified, source safety confirmed. See `admin/ANDRE_SOLUTION_PROPOSAL.md`.

## Current ‚ÄĒ July 2 (CloudLab campaign, 4 weeks to July 31)

**Where the science stands** (full synthesis: `docs/cloudlab/RECEIVE_PATH_FINDINGS.md`):

- **Mechanism proven** on real 10G hardware: ~27‚Äď33% of NAPI polls wasted, 95‚Äď99.7% of them
  MISSED-driven re-polls on an UNCRYPTED head.
- **Two-sided fix (wg_supp + wg_headwake) halves wasted polls** ~27% ‚Üí ~14%, flat 8‚Äď64 peers.
- **Throughput lever is parallelism, not the fix**: `sdfn` NIC hash spreads receive over 8
  cores, 4.1 ‚Üí 9.0 Gb/s (√ó2.2) with stock WireGuard.
- **Phase A (sub-saturation, 64 runs): clean CPU null on c220g2**; latency trend favorable
  but noisy + power-state-confounded ‚ÄĒ not claimed.
- **Phase B next**: decrypt-cost sweep (`measure_decrypt_sweep.sh`, rewritten 2026-07-02 to
  capped-load single-window design) ‚ÄĒ the remaining place a user-visible win could live.
  Then optional governor=performance latency re-test, and the headwake reliability soak.
